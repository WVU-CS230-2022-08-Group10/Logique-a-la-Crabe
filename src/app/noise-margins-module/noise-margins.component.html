Noise Margins

When signals are pushed through a current they can collect voltage noise by the signal either dropping or raising. Noise margins are the area where signals can be pushed through a circuit safely.

First, we need to define some variables

VDD is the high signal with a binary value of 1

GND (ground) is the low signal with a binary value of 0

VOH is voltage output high and VOL is voltage output low

VIH is voltage input high and VIL is voltage input low

NMH is noise margin high and NML is noise margin low

To calculate the the noies margin high value use the formula... NMH = VOH - VIH

To calculate the noise margin low value use the formula... NML = VIL - VOL

Both NMH and NML need to be postive for a gate to drive another gate
